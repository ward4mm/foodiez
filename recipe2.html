Quesadilla with Corn Salsa

Ingredients

☺ 2 Tortillas

☺ 1/2 c Mexican Cheese

☺ 1 can Corn

☺ 2 Jalapenos

☺ 1/2 Red Onion

☺ 1/4 c Cilantro

☺ Juice of 1 Lime

☺ 1/2 tsp Salt, Pepper, and Garlic

Directions

Corn Salsa

Begin by straining your canned corn with a colander or strainer. Leave that in the colander and start chopping your Jalapenos. After you finish choping your jalapenos, finely chop your red onion and cilantro.

Butter or oil a medium sized on medium to high heat pan and then add your corn, for extra flavor, you can add salt and garlic powder. Let your corn sit and stir every 1-2 minutes until you start to see it blacken. After 4 stirs, add to a mixing bowl and combine with chopped red onion, jalapenos, cilantro, and the juice of 1 lime.

Quesadilla

Once your corn salsa is ready, it's time to assemble your quesadilla! Butter or oil your pan on low to medium heat and sprinkle some garlic and salt on the bottom of the pan (this adds a lot of flavor!). Next place one tortilla on the bottom of the pan. Add 1/4 a cup of cheese (or as much as you want) to the bottom portion of the quesadilla. Then, add a couple spoonfuls of your corn salsa on top of the cheese. Next, top it off with anoth 1/4 cup of cheese and place your second tortilla on top.

Put a lid over your quesadilla in order to help the cheese melt, but keep an eye on it as it cooks. Once it appears light brown on the bottom (you can use a spatula to lift it up and check), place a large plate over the top of the pan. Pick up the pan with the plate on top and flip it over! Your quesadilla should now be on your plate browned-side up! Use a spatula to slide it back into the pan and give it a minute or two to brown on the other side!
