Tips for Grocery Shopping in College

Find your staples!

Staples are a great thing to have in your kitchen and not everyone's picks will be the same. Make a list of all of your favorite meals and their ingrdients.Pick out the ingredients that show up the most in your meals. Pick a protein, starch, and vegetable, those can become your staples! For example, my favorite meals are usually veggies, chicken, and some sort of starch, quesadillas, avocado toast with eggs, and stirfry. A protein that would work well in all of those is chicken, a veggie could be tri-colored peppers, and a starch would be rice! Obviously I'd probably have to get more ingredients for each dish, but the idea is to cut down the amount of food you have to buy, while still having many options.

Buy What YOU Want.

It's so easy to feel like you have to buy what your friends buy or eat what your friends or rommates eat. But remember, you're the on eating this food, buy what appeals to you. I live with three rommates who barely grocery shop. Sometimes I feel like I'm wasting money and time by grocery shopping and cooking every week when I could just eat what's in the cabinet or whatever is there. Cooking is something I love to do and I'm more of a meal person in comaprison to snacking, and that's okay. Whether it's a while bunch of veggies or a five containers of oreos, you do you!

Download the App

Nowadays, most grocery strores have apps that you can download where you create an account and recieve perks! They also make it easier for you to download coupons and order your groceries for pickup. Not only does this save money but it can save you so much time! Whether it be picking out your list of groceries, finding where items are located or simply picking your groceries up with ease!
